PrefaceArticle 4.3 and 4.4 of this standard are compulsory terms, and others are commendatory.The Standard replaces GB 19153-2003 Minimum allowable values of energy efficiency and Evaluating values of energy conservation for displacement air compressors.The Standard has the following main changes, compared with GB 19153-2003:--The Name of the standard is changed to Minimum allowable values of energy efficiency and energy efficiency grades for displacement air compressors.--Add efficiency grades and target minimum allowable values of energy efficiency.--Add oil-injection single-screw air compressor in the scope.The standard is proposed by Standardization Technical Committee, National Energy Basis and Management.The standard is controlled by Standardization Technical Committee, National Energy Basis and Management. Minimum allowable values of energy efficiency and energy efficiency grades for displacement air compressors1 ScopeThe standard is established to define the efficiency grades, efficiency limit, target minimum allowable values of energy efficiency, evaluating values of energy conservation, test methods and inspection rules of the displacement air compressors (hereinafter referred as Air Compressor).2. Reference DocumentsThe following articles are part of the Standard after being referenced. All amendment sheets (excluding wrong printings) and revisions of the reference documents which are not marked with date will not be applicable to this standard. Yet it is encouraged that agreement parties shall investigate whether the latest version of these documents are applicable. The latest version of all reference documents without date is not applicable to this standard.GB/T 3853 Displacement compressor--Acceptance tests (GB/T 3853-1998, eqv ISO 1217:1996) GB/T 4975 Displacement compressors vocabulary--General (GB/T 4975-1995, eqv ISO 3857:1977)GB/T 13279GB/T 13928 JB/T 4253 JB/T 6430 JB/T 7662 JB/T 8933 JB/T 8934 JB/T 9107 JB/T 10525Stationary reciprocating piston air compressor for general useReciprocating piston micro air compressorsOil flooded sliding vane air compressor for general useOil injected screw air compressor for general use Displacement compressor vocabulary-Rotating compressor Oil–free reciprocating piston air compressorsDirect drive portable reciprocating piston air compressors Reciprocating Compressors VocabularyOil injected single screw air compressor for general use3. Terminology and definitionTerminology and definitions specified in GB/T 3853, GB/T 4975, JB/T 7662 and JB/T 9107, and the following terminologies are applicable to the standard.3.1 Actual volume rate of flow of an air compressorThe volume flow rate in standard air discharge position by the air discharged by the air compressor. This flow rate shall be converted to full temperature, full pressure and constituents state (e.g. humidity) in standard suction position, and the unit is m3/min.3.2 Input power of air compressorThe total input power of the air compressor under rated power supply (e.g. phase, voltage, frequency), the unit is kW.3.3 Input specific powerUnder referenced working conditions, the rate between the input power and actual volume flow of the air compressor, the unit is Kw/(m3/min)3.4 Minimum allowable values of energy efficiency for air compressorsUnder referenced working conditions, the max. allowable input power rate of the air compressor
3.5 Target minimum allowable values of energy efficiency for air compressorsAfter the certain years after implementation of the standard, the allowable max. input power rate, this value replaces the target minimum allowable values of energy efficiency after the standard is implemented.3.6 Evaluating values of energy conservation for air compressorThe allowable max. input power rate of the Energy-conservation air compressor under regulated working conditions.4. Technical Requirements4.1 Basic requirementsThe general and safety performance of the air compressor shall conform to GB/T 13270, GB/T 13928, GB/T 4253, JB/T 6430, JB/T 8933, JB/T 8934, JB/T 10525, etc.4.2 Efficiency gradesThere are 3 grades: Grade 1, the highest. The grades shall conform to Table 1-7.4.3 Minimum allowable values of energy efficiencyThe value shall not exceed that in Table 1-74.4 Target minimum allowable values of energy efficiencyThe value shall not exceed that in Table 1-7. The value will replace that in 4.3 after 4 years since implementation of the standard.4.5 Evaluating values of energy conservationThe value shall not exceed that in Grade 2 of Table 1-7.Table 1.1 Direct drive portable reciprocating oil injection piston air compressors energy consumption level                                   Working pressure Mpa     0.25  0.4  0.5 0.7  0.8 1.0   Compressor Special Energy Requirement  KW/(m3/min)                           0.25      18.8  10.5  11.513.2  14 15.5  29.3  11  12.113.9  14.7 16.3  311.6  13.8  15.117.4  18.4 20.4  T10.5  12.5  13.7 15.8  16.7 18.5   0.37      18.8  10.5 11.513.2  14 15.5  29.3  11  12.113.9  14.7 16.3  311.6  13.8  15.117.4  18.4 20.4  T10.5  12.5  13.715.8  16.7 18.5   0.55      18.6  10.1  10.912.6  13.4 14.9  29  10.6  11.5 13.3  14.1 15.7  311.2  13.3 14.416.6  17.6 19.6 T10.2   12.1   13.1 15.1   16 17.8                         1 stageCompressor StageDriven motor normal power (kW)Energy Consumption level

Rules for inspection6.1 Delivery inspectionThe inspection shall be done according to GB/T 13279, GB/T 13928, JB/T 4253, JB/T 6430, JB/T 8933, JB/T 8934 or JB/T 10525.6.2 Routine InspectionThe routine inspection for input power rate shall be done at least once a year, and sample shall be taken at random from the qualified products through delivery inspection, the spot sampling shall accord with GB/T 13279, GB/T 13928, JB/T 4253, JB/T 6430, JB/T 8933, JB/T 8934 or JB/T 10525. Before the routine inspection, all samples shall be inspected for Minimum allowable values of energy efficiency, Any nonconforming products found shall be replaced with qualified ones, and meanwhile, the failure reason shall be analyzed and recorded, but shall not be regarded as evidence for routine inspection results.Routine inspection shall also be done if one of the following occurs:a) During appraisal of the design finalization before trial-productionb) Production recovered again after 3-month downc) Any change of the design, process or materials may influence the product performanced) Proposed by quality supervision department.
